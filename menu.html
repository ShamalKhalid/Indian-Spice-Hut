Breads, Dosas and Combos

Breads

Porotta

A popular South Indian flatbread known for its flaky layers and soft texture. Made from unleavened dough, it's typically enjoyed with curries or gravies. The unique preparation method gives Porotta its distinctive taste, making it a versatile and beloved choice in South Indian cuisine.

Naan

A traditional Indian flatbread, soft and pillowy, typically cooked in a tandoor (clay oven). This versatile bread complements various dishes, from curries to kebabs, and is loved for its fluffy texture and slight char. A staple in Indian cuisine, Naan is a perfect accompaniment to savor the flavors of a diverse range of meals.

Appam

A South Indian delicacy, Appam is a lacy, fermented rice pancake with a soft center and crisp edges. Prepared using a batter of rice and coconut milk, these bowl-shaped delights are known for their unique texture and subtle sweetness. Often served with coconut milk or spicy curries, Appam is a delightful addition to any meal.

Chapati

Chapati, a whole wheat flatbread, offers nutritional benefits. Rich in fiber, it aids digestion, helps maintain weight, and provides sustained energy. Its wholesome nature supports heart health and contributes essential nutrients to a balanced diet.

Poori

A deep-fried, puffed Indian bread made from unleavened wheat dough. Crispy on the outside and soft inside, Poori is a versatile accompaniment to various dishes. Often enjoyed with curries, pickles, or sweet accompaniments, it adds a delightful texture to the dining experience.

Dosa

Masala dosa

A South Indian classic, Masala Dosa is a thin and crispy rice crepe filled with a spiced potato mixture. This popular dish is often accompanied by coconut chutney and tangy sambar. Known for its delicious contrast of textures and flavors, Masala Dosa is a beloved choice for breakfast or a satisfying meal.

Mysore Masala Dosa

A flavorful variation of the traditional masala dosa, Mysore Masala Dosa features a spicy red chutney spread on the crispy rice crepe before being filled with the aromatic spiced potato mixture. This delightful twist adds an extra layer of heat and flavor, making it a popular and savory choice in South Indian cuisine.

Ghee roast

A mouthwatering South Indian specialty where a dosa (rice crepe) is generously smeared with ghee (clarified butter) and cooked until crispy. The result is a golden, aromatic dosa with a rich, buttery flavor. Often served with coconut chutney and sambar, Ghee Roast Dosa is a delightful indulgence for dosa enthusiasts.

Plain dosa

A classic South Indian delight, Plain Dosa is a thin and crispy rice crepe made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). Light and versatile, it is typically served with coconut chutney and sambar, offering a simple yet delightful eating experience.

Thattu Dosa Set

A street food variant, featuring dosas cooked on flat griddles (thattu), typically served with various chutneys and accompaniments.

Idli set

(Served 4 pieces in a set)
A staple South Indian dish, idli is a steamed rice cake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). These soft and spongy cakes are light, gluten-free, and typically served with coconut chutney and sambar, making them a popular and healthy breakfast or snack option.
